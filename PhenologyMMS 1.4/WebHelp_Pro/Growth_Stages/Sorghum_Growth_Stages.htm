Sorghum Growth Stages

Sorghum has eleven growth stages depicted in PhenologyMMS which follow those of winter wheat with a few differences.

 

A more generalized view of sorghum divides its growth into three stages. The first stage (GS I In Sorghum, some view the growth stages in a more generalized manner breaking the growth of the plant into three main stages. This is Growth Stage I and refers to the time in which the plant is experiencing vegetative growth.) is when vegetative growth occurs, the second stage (GS II In Sorghum, some view the growth stages in a more generalized manner breaking the growth of the plant into three main stages. This is Growth Stage II and refers to the time when the reproductive structures are being formed.) is when the reproductive structures are formed and the third stage (GS III In Sorghum, some view the growth stages in a more generalized manner breaking the growth of the plant into three main stages. This is Growth Stage III and refers to the time when the grain is ripening.) is devoted to grain ripening The changes in a fruit following seed maturation which prepares the fruit to disperse its seed..

E to First Tiller Additional lateral shoots formed from axillary buds. They emerge from the axils of the true leaves at the base of the main stem. They can contribute to the final yield of the plant however, not all tillers produce grain. - the first tiller emerges and occurs at the four and six-leaf stage.

 

E to Internode Elongation Starts The space between the nodes on the stem of a plant. When these spaces elongate or increase the distance between nodes, the stem grows longer. Abbreviated by 'IES' Internode Elongation Starts. (IES Abbreviation for 'Internode Elongation Starts' growth stage. The spaces between the nodes i.e., internodes, begin to increase the distance between the nodes and the stem grows longer.) - the internode begins to elongate.

 

IES to Jointing A growth stage in grasses when the stem begins lengthening as a result of internode elongation and the first node becomes visible just above the soil line or at the base of the shoot. This is abbreviated as 'J'. (J) - jointing occurs when the first node Enlarged areas on the stem where active cell division takes place. First Bloom A flower. The first reproductive stage in Dry Beans abbreviated as R1. AS to Half Bloom In Sorghum, the growth stage when flowering has progressed halfway down the head; also, when 50% of the plants in the field are in some stage of bloom. It is abbreviated as 'HB'. (HB Abbreviation for the 'Half Bloom' stage in Sorghum. HB to Full Bloom In Sorghum, when the panicle is fully flowered or when all the plants in the field are in some stage of bloom. This is noted in the Growth Stages screen and Phenology and Developmental Sequence diagrams as FB. (FB In Sorghum. the abbreviation for the growth stage when the heads have fully flowered; the panicle has fully bloomed.) - the heads of the plants are fully bloomed.
